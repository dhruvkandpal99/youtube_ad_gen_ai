# Muji - Brand Style Guide

## Brand Tone & Voice
The tone of Muji is calm, quiet, intentional, and understated. We speak with simplicity and honesty, avoiding hyperbole, buzzwords, or aggressive sales tactics. Our messaging focus is on the utility, quality, and natural origin of the product. The writing style is reflective, mindful, and concise—valuing silence and breathing room as much as the words themselves. We don't yell; we speak softly and with clarity.

## Color Palette
Our visual assets should utilize a muted, harmonious color scheme that reflects natural materials and peaceful workspaces:
- **Primary**: Warm Grey, Off-White, Natural Kraft Tan
- **Secondary**: Charcoal, Soft Beige, Muted Sage Green

## Visual Style & Creative Direction
All photography and video assets must feature:
- **Flat Lays & Clean Surfaces**: Notebooks arranged neatly on wooden desks, linen fabrics, or concrete textures.
- **Natural Daylight**: Soft, diffuse side-lighting that creates gentle, long shadows. Avoid artificial studio flashes or harsh, direct spotlights.
- **Generous Negative Space**: Allow the product to breathe by surrounding it with empty space. The composition should feel calm and uncrowded.
- **Mindful Interaction**: Subtle human presence is welcome, such as a hand writing with a pen, but the focus must remain on the quiet, tactile experience.

## What to Avoid
- **Busy or Cluttered Backgrounds**: Do not place products on messy desks, busy patterns, or colorful backdrops.
- **Bright or Neon Colors**: Avoid high-saturation colors, flashing lights, or digital aesthetics.
- **High-Tech Environments**: Avoid placing notebooks next to glowing screens, cables, or modern gaming setups.
- **Hype-Driven Advertising**: Avoid bold exclamation points, neon typography, or sensational marketing slogans.

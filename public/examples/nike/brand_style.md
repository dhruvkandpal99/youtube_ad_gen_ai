# Nike Trail - Brand Style Guide

## Brand Tone & Voice
The tone of Nike Trail is bold, raw, grit-filled, and deeply aspirational. We speak directly to the inner drive of the athlete. Our copy should feel authentic, intense, and grounded in the reality of physical struggle. We celebrate the process of training, the dirt, the rain, and the endurance required to conquer the outdoors. The voice is never overly polished or corporate; it is real, motivational, and urgent.

## Color Palette
Our visual assets should utilize a strong, energetic color scheme reflecting both the natural elements and athletic energy:
- **Primary**: Deep Obsidian Black, Summit White
- **Accents**: Volt Green (representing energy and visibility), University Red (representing passion, heartbeat, and grit)

## Visual Style & Creative Direction
All photography and video assets must feature:
- **Action Shots**: Athletes in motion, capturing the physical exertion, sweat, and intensity of trail running.
- **Natural Light**: Dawn, dusk, overcast skies, or harsh midday sun. Shadows and highlights should feel natural and unedited.
- **Real Elements**: Rain, mud, dirt, dust, and rugged mountain backdrops are encouraged. The environment should feel like a character in the creative.
- **Raw Presentation**: Focus on the details of the athlete's struggle—mud on the legs, sweat on the brow, and dirt on the shoes.

## What to Avoid
- **Posed Studio Photography**: Avoid clean, static poses in front of seamless backdrops.
- **Plain White Backgrounds**: Do not showcase the shoe in isolated, sanitised environments.
- **Overly Clean Aesthetic**: Avoid pristine gear, perfectly clean shoes, or models who do not look like they are actively training.
- **Soft or Cozy Visuals**: The visual tone must be intense and active, not relaxing or passive.
